My Dissertation:

The Processing and Classification of EEG Vowel Signals in Imagined Speech.

Deep Learning Project, ML sub-set.

Abstract


This report is a response to the previous report by C. Nguyen, G. Karavas, P. Artemiadis on,
“Inferred Imagined Speech using EEG signals: a new approach using Riemannian Manifold features",
Journal of Neural Engineering, July 2017 [1].
It confirms that Imagined speech has potential within BCI.
This report makes an investigation on how to optimise and whether or not, 
this research can justify the decoding of EEG signals from coma patients.
The design aspect comes from the manipulation of the LSTM architecture layers and focuses on an investigation of hyperparameters,
and parameters for optimization of vowel sound training when a patient is in resting condition, only.
Vowel classification is improved upon from Nguyen, Artemiadis and Karavas’ work [1].
